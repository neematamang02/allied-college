History

Background

Allied College, a prestigious educational institution, has a rich and illustrious history that spans several decades. Founded in 1950, the college emerged as a result of the collective vision and dedication of a group of renowned educators and philanthropists.
The college was established with the primary goal of providing high-quality education and fostering intellectual growth among its students. From its inception, Allied College set out to create an inclusive and supportive environment, where students from diverse backgrounds could come together to pursue their academic ambitions. During its early years, Allied College faced numerous challenges as it worked towards establishing itself as a leading educational institution. However, through the relentless efforts of its faculty and staff, the college steadily gained recognition for its commitment to academic excellence and innovative teaching methods.
In the 1960s and 1970s, Allied College underwent significant expansion, with the addition of new academic departments, faculty members, and state-of-the-art facilities. This period saw the college diversify its curriculum and introduce specialized programs in various fields, catering to the evolving needs of the student body. The 1980s marked a turning point for Allied College, as it embraced technological advancements and incorporated them into its educational framework. The college became an early adopter of computer-based learning, establishing computer labs and integrating technology into the curriculum. This forward-thinking approach helped Allied College stay ahead of the curve and prepare its students for the digital age.
In the following decades, Allied College continued to grow and evolve. It forged partnerships with leading industry organizations, fostering internship and job placement opportunities for its students. The college also prioritized research and innovation, encouraging faculty and students to engage in groundbreaking research projects that contributed to advancements in their respective fields.
